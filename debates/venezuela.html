The Premise

Context

The debate focuses on the US seizure of Nicolas Maduro. Was it a victory for hemispheric security or a violation of the law?

Anthony (YAL)

Argues from a Constitutionalist perspective, emphasizing Congressional war powers and the risks of long-term peacekeeping.

Will (Republican)

Advocates for a resurgent Monroe Doctrine, citing national security and the use of force as the ultimate jurisdiction.

Detailed Debate Record 
[00:01:21] ON CONSTITUTIONAL PROCESS
Anthony: You have to do it by the Constitution. We are not a country of "whatever the last guy did." The AUMF is vested within the legislative branch.

Will: The legislature has implemented policy to allow the President to engage in limited strikes. This was a clean execution of police authority. Maduro was an illegitimate leader whose actions led to American deaths.

[00:09:21] ON GLOBAL JURISDICTION
Anthony: To what extent does the US have jurisdiction outside its own borders?

Will: The only thing that matters in the state of nature is the use of force. The US military has no competition. Therefore, the United States is the ultimate jurisdiction over the entire world.

Anthony: To presume we have authority over other nations simply because we have a military able to enforce it puts us in dangerous moral territory.

Will: Security of the American people is the only thing that matters. I am not concerned about the morality of pushing around third-world nations if it preserves the security of the American people.
