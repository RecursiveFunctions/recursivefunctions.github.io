Venezuela Intervention Debate

Constitutionalists vs. Interventionists

Debate Video
READY

The Premise

Moderator

US forces have seized Venezuelan leader Nicolas Maduro and brought him to the United States. We are here to debate: Was this action justified? Is this the "America First" way?

YAL (Anthony)

Anthony Cacciato represents the Constitutionalist/Skeptic view. He questions the legality of the intervention and the long-term consequences of "policing" the hemisphere.

REPUBLICAN (Will)

William Branson Donaghue represents the Interventionist/Hawk view. He advocates for a revived Monroe Doctrine and absolute US dominance in the Western Hemisphere.

Topic 1: Constitutionality & War Powers

Does the President have the authority to seize a foreign leader?

YAL (Anthony) - Argument

  • We are a country of laws, not "whatever the last guy did."
  • Intervention requires a Congressional Authorization for Use of Military Force (AUMF).
  • Checks and balances require input from elected representatives, not just the Executive branch.

REPUBLICAN (Will) - Response

  • The President has "Police Power" for limited strikes regarding national security.
  • This wasn't a war; it was a "clean execution" of police authority to abduct a criminal.
  • Maduro was pushing drugs that killed Americans; removing him is a law enforcement action.

Topic 2: Jurisdiction & "The Empire"

Does the US have jurisdiction over Venezuela?

YAL (Anthony) - Argument

  • Does the US have legal jurisdiction outside its own borders?
  • Presuming authority simply because we have the military to enforce it puts us in "dangerous moral territory."
  • The Monroe Doctrine was about self-determination, not US overlordship.

REPUBLICAN (Will) - Response

  • "The only thing that matters... is the use of force."
  • The US military has no competition; therefore, the US is the "ultimate jurisdiction over the entire world."
  • International law is just "words on paper." The only ones who can stop us is us.
  • Morality is secondary to security; we must lock down the hemisphere against China/Russia.

Topic 3: Historical Precedent

Panama vs. Iraq: The Intervention Model

REPUBLICAN (Will) - Argument

  • 1947 was the turning point where we wrongly became "World Police" (Globalism).
  • Neocon wars (Iraq, Libya) failed because we tried to build democracies.
  • We should emulate Panama (1989) and Grenada: Swift, decisive, restore order, leave.
  • Venezuela will be a quick win like Panama, not a quagmire like Iraq.

YAL (Anthony) - Rebuttal

  • Even swift interventions risk "stabilization work" (peacekeeping).
  • "You cannot govern by Predator drone." You need boots on the ground.
  • Without a clear plan, we risk sinking American lives into another Middle East-style occupation.

Q&A Highlights

On Risking American Lives

"Will, you say 'America First,' but putting troops in Venezuela risks American lives. How does that jibe?"

REPUBLICAN (Will)

  • "Abroad" means Europe/Asia. The Americas are our backyard.
  • We should have bases in Venezuela, Chile, and El Salvador to block China.
  • "The Venezuelan people are happy we liberated them."
  • We should occupy this long-term.

YAL (Anthony)

  • Troops on the ground leads to peacekeeping, which leads to quagmires.
  • Congress needs to authorize this specific risk via AUMF.
